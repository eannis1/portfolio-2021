Martini

History

"The exact lineage of the martini is unknown, but it may have evolved from another cocktail created in Martinez, California, during the mid-1800s gold rush. As the story goes, a miner who had struck gold mosied into a bar for celebratory Champagne, but there wasn’t any, so the bartender used what he had on-hand — gin, vermouth, maraschino liqueur, bitters and a lemon slice — and called it the Martinez Special."

From Taylor Rock, "The Fascinating Origins of Your Favorite Cocktails," August 27, 2019

Dry Martini

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ ounces gin
  • ½ ounce dry vermouth
  • 1 dash orange bitters
  • Garnish: lemon twist

Needed Tools

  • Mixing glass
  • Cocktail glass
  • Strainer

Find the recipe at Liquor.com's Dry Martini.

Dirty Martini

Exchange orange bitters for olive brine to create the salty, savory dirty martini.

Ingredients

  • 2 ½ ounces gin or vodka
  • ½ ounce dry vermouth
  • ½ ounce olive brine
  • Garnish: 2 to 4 olives

Needed Tools

  • Mixing glass
  • Cocktail glass
  • Strainer

Find the recipe at Liquor.com's Dirty Martini.

Espresso Martini

For a sweeter take on the martini that also packs a caffeine punch, combine espresso, coffee liqueur, and simple syrup with vodka.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ ounces vodka
  • 1 ounce freshly brewed espresso coffee or cold brew concentrate
  • 1 ounce coffee liqueur (such as Kahlua)
  • ½ ounce simple syrup
  • Garnish: 4 coffee beans

Needed Tools

  • Cocktail shaker
  • Cocktail glass
  • Strainer

Find the recipe at BBC Good Food's Espresso Martini.

Zucchini Martini

Though zucchini in a martini seems odd, steeping grated zucchini in lemon juice and sugar allows it to pair beautifully with the vermouth and gin and produces a refreshing, unusual drink.

Ingredients

  • ½ zucchini
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 ounce vermouth
  • 1 ½ ounces gin
  • Garnish: mint leaf

Needed Tools

  • Coarse grater
  • Cocktail shaker
  • Cocktail glass
  • Strainer

Find the recipe at BBC Good Food's Courgette (Zucchini) Martini.
