History

"The Pendennis Club in Louisville, Kentucky, has long been touted as the old fashioned’s birthplace. Here, people asked for a cocktail 'made the old-fashioned way,' which, to many, meant adding sugar and bitters to make harsh alcohol (in this case, bourbon) taste better. Over time, people started muddling oranges and cherries into it, too."

From Taylor Rock, "The Fascinating Origins of Your Favorite Cocktails," August 27, 2019

Old Fashioned

Though the recipe seems simple, combining Angostura bitters with rye or bourbon produces a drink that hovers between bitter and sweet.

Ingredients

  • 1 sugar cube or ¼ ounce simple syrup
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • 2 ounces rye or bourbon
  • Garnish: orange twist

Needed Tools

  • Rocks glass

Find the recipe at New York Times' Old Fashioned.

Maple Syrup Old Fashioned

Replacing simple syrup with maple syrup gives this Old Fashioned recipe a deceptively complex flavor twist.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces bourbon
  • ½ to 1 teaspoon maple syrup
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • Garnish: orange peel and cocktail cherry

Needed Tools

  • Lowball glass

Find the recipe at Cookie and Kate's Best Old Fashioned.

Irish Old Fashioned

Combining a base of Irish whiskey with the bright citrus notes of orange bitters and the herbal tones of Benadictine adds depth to the traditional Old Fashioned.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces Irish whiskey
  • ¾ ounces Benadictine
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • 2 dashes orange bitters
  • Garnish: orange twist

Needed Tools

  • Mixing glass
  • Rocks glass

Find the recipe at Liquor.com's Irish Old Fashioned.

Smoking Rosemary Old Fashioned

Infusing a classic Old Fashioned with smoke from burning rosemary not only adds herbal flavors to the drink, but also gives it a smoky tang.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces bourbon or rye
  • ½ ounce simple syrup
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • 2 pieces dried rosemary
  • Garnish: orange peel

Needed Tools

  • Cocktail shaker
  • Match or lighter
  • Lowball or rocks glass small enough for the shaker to fit over

Find the recipe at Town and Country Magazine's Smoking Rosemary Old Fashioned.
