History

"As the name suggests, this drink is rumored to have originated at the Manhattan Club in New York City in the early 1870s. Dr. Iain Marshall made it to serve at a banquet honoring then-governor and presidential candidate Samuel J. Tilden. The first-ever recipe called for equal parts vermouth and whiskey, and a dash of orange bitters. Today, it’s made with two parts rye, one part sweet vermouth, a dash of bitters and a cherry garnish."

From Taylor Rock, "The Fascinating Origins of Your Favorite Cocktails," August 27, 2019

Classic Manhattan

A combination of sweet and dry vermouth soften the rye whiskey, and Angostura bitters add complex herbal flavors to the classic Manhattan.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces rye whiskey
  • ½ ounce dry vermouth
  • ½ ounce sweet vermouth
  • 2 dashes Angostura bitters
  • Garnish: brandied cherry or lemon twist

Needed Tools

  • Mixing glass
  • Strainer
  • Cocktail glass

Find the recipe at Liquor.com's Manhattan.

Black Manhattan

Replacing vermouth with Averno amaro and adding orange bitters produces the Black Manhattan, a darker, more herbal version of the classic Manhattan.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces rye whiskey
  • 1 ounce Averno amaro
  • 1 dash Angostura bitters
  • 1 dash orange bitters
  • Garnish: cherry

Needed Tools

  • Mixing glass
  • Strainer
  • Cocktail glass

Find the recipe at Bevvy's Black Manhattan.

Greenpoint

Adding mild, sweet yellow Chartreuse, made by monks in France from an ancient recipe, to the Manhattan recipe produces the Greenpoint, a subtle drink with layers of flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces rye whiskey
  • 1 ounce Punt e Mes vermouth
  • 1 teaspoon yellow Chartreuse
  • 1 dash Angostura bitters

Needed Tools

  • Cocktail shaker
  • Strainer
  • Champagne coupe

Find the recipe at Saveur's Greenpoint.

Clint Eastwood

Green Chartreuse and a heavy dose of bitters combine with, but do not overwhelm, rye whiskey to create a drink with powerful flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces rye whiskey
  • ¼ ounce demerara syrup
  • ⅛ ounce Angostura bitters
  • ⅛ ounce Green Chartreuse

Needed Tools

  • Cocktail shaker
  • Strainer
  • Cocktail glass

Find the recipe at The Humble Garnish's Clint Eastwood.
