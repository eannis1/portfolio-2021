History

"The Whiskey Sour officially dates back to the 1860s, but sailors in the British Navy had been drinking something very similar long before that" combining liquor, the juice of lemons or limes, sugar, and water to combat bad water and scurvy. "When it comes to the official record, there are three main points of reference for the Whiskey Sour. The first written record comes in the seminal 1862 book The Bartender’s Guide: How To Mix Drinks by Jerry Thomas.... The next reference comes from, of all places, an 1870 edition of the Waukesha Plainsdealer, a Wisconsin newspaper. The final reference to the drink comes two years later, in 1872. A former ship steward, Elliot Staub, 'invented' a drink — the whiskey sour — in a bar in Iquique (then part of Peru). Through these three origins, we come to, more or less, what we have today–a cocktail that mixes a spirit, a sour, and a sweet."

From Sam Slaughter, "A Brief History of the Whiskey Sour," May 25, 2020

Classic Whiskey Sour (no egg white)

Bourbon and lemon juice combine in the classic Whiskey Sour to produce a perfect mix of flavors.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces bourbon
  • ¾ ounce fresh lemon juice
  • ¾ ounce simple syrup
  • Garnish: ½ orange wheel and Maraschino cherry

Needed Tools

  • Cocktail shaker
  • Hawthorne strainer or slotted spoon
  • Rocks glass

Find the recipe at Bon Appetit's Whiskey Sour.

Classic Whiskey Sour (with egg white)

Adding an egg white to the classic whiskey sour recipe produces a beautiful foam.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces bourbon
  • 1 ounce fresh lemon juice
  • ¾ ounce simple syrup
  • 1 egg white from a small to medium egg
  • Garnish: Maraschino cherry

Needed Tools

  • Cocktail shaker
  • Hawthorne strainer
  • Rocks or coupe glass

Find the recipe at The Kitchn's Whiskey Sour.

Blackberry Whiskey Sour

Honey, blackberries, and ginger beer add the flavors of summer to the traditional Whiskey Sour.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces bourbon
  • 1 ounce lemon juice
  • 1 ounce ginger beer
  • ½ ounce honey
  • ½ cup blackberries
  • Garnish: blackberries and mint leaf

Needed Tools

  • Mixing glass
  • Strainer
  • Rocks or other desired glass

Find the recipe at Advanced Mixology's Blackberry Whiskey Sour.

New York Sour

Combining two kinds of cognac, two kinds of citrus, and red wine produces a complex, sophisticated drink worthy of New York City.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces cognac
  • Martin VSOP or Hennessy Black
  • ¾ ounces fresh orange juice
  • ¾ ounces fresh lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons superfine sugar
  • ½ ounce dry red wine

Needed Tools

  • Cocktail shaker
  • Strainer
  • Spoon
  • Champagne coupe or other glass

Find the recipe at Saveur's New York Sour.

Scottish Dream Whiskey Sour

Grapefruit juice and fig bitters lighten the cinnamon simple syrup and whiskey in this Scottish-inspired take on a Whiskey Sour.

Ingredients

  • Cinnamon simple syrup (see link below for recipe)
  • 1 ½ ounce blended whiskey
  • 1 ounce grapefruit juice
  • ½ ounce cinnamon syrup
  • ½ ounce lemon juice
  • 2 dashes Black Mission Fig bitters

Needed Tools

  • Saucepan for making the cinnamon simple syrup
  • Cocktail shaker
  • Strainer
  • Tumbler

Find the recipe at Saveur's Scottish Dream Whiskey Sour.
