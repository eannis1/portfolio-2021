Moscow Mule

History

One might assume that the Moscow mule hails from Russia, but it was actually invented in Los Angeles, California. One night in 1941, a man named John Martin (president of liquor company Heublein) walked into his friend Jack Morgan’s bar, the Cock ‘n’ Bull. The bartender there, Wes Price, was trying to get rid of ginger beer because no one was buying it, and Martin’s company had just acquired Smirnoff, so he had a surplus of vodka. They put the two together and it was an instant hit. As for the famous mug, Morgan’s girlfriend, Osalene Schmitt, inherited a copper goods business, so they deemed it a match made in heaven (and very effective marketing).

From Taylor Rock, "The Fascinating Origins of Your Favorite Cocktails," August 27, 2019

Classic Moscow Mule

Combining vodka with lime juice and ginger beer produces a drink that would bring cheer even to the heart of a Russian winter.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces vodka
  • ½ ounce fresh lime juice
  • 3 ounces ginger beer
  • Garnish: lime wedge

Needed Tools

  • Cocktail shaker
  • Strainer
  • Copper Moscow mule mug

Kentucky Mule

Using bourbon rather than vodka as a base smoothes the flavor profile of a Kentucky mule when compared to the classic Moscow mule, resulting in a drink that combines the best of both zest and depth.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces bourbon
  • ½ ounce fresh lime juice
  • 4-6 ounces ginger beer

Needed Tools

  • Collins glass or Moscow mule mug

Find the recipe at Food Network's Kentucky Mule.

London Mule

Replacing the foundation of vodka with gin produces a drink that combines the roughness of gin with the zest of lime juice and ginger beer into a medley that would be at home in all levels of London society.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces gin
  • ½ ounce lime juice, freshly squeezed
  • 3 ounces ginger beer
  • Garnish: lime wheel

Needed Tools

  • Copper Moscow mule mug or highball glass

Find the recipe at Liquor.com's London Mule.

Dark 'N Stormy

Though many debate whether a Dark ‘N Stormy is truly a mule, using dark rum as a base and lightening it with lime juice and ginger beer creates a sweet and spicy drink that not many would turn down.

Ingredients

  • 2 ounces dark rum
  • ½ ounce lime juice, freshly squeezed
  • 3 ounces ginger beer
  • Garnish: candied ginger slice and lime wheel

Needed Tools

  • Cocktail shaker
  • Strainer
  • Collins glass

Find the recipe at Food and Wine's Dark 'N Stormy.
